01 — About

Who I am


Over six years ago, I started building things before anyone asked for them: communities, machine learning solutions, intelligent systems, platforms, and ecosystems. Today, they have scaled to reach and impact more than 30,000 people across 80+ countries

I am technical enough to build and architect the systems and human enough to know that technology means nothing if the right people cannot access it. That tension between what is possible and who it actually serves is what drives everything I do.

At Iterative and DagsHub, I worked remotely with teams across Europe, North America, and Asia, helping developers trust new tools, adopt new workflows, and build better AI systems for their business responsibly. At CBB Analytics, I leveraged machine learning, data science, engineering and analytics to solve real business problems, turning data into actionable insights that drove meaningful impact and improved decision-making. At Data Community Africa, I went deep on something harder to measure but impossible to ignore: what happens when an entire continent of builders finally has a room of their own?

Beyond the technical work, I speak at global conferences, create technical content, and consistently champion a vision for AI and data that is more accessible, inclusive, and impactful.

If you are building something that needs to last — technically sound, community-rooted, and built for the world as it actually is, that is the work I show up for.
