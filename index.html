About

Technical enough to build the system. Human enough to ask who it serves.

Over six years ago, I started building things before anyone asked for them: communities, machine learning solutions, platforms, and ecosystems. What started as side projects now serves a global audience across 120+ countries.

At Iterative and DagsHub, I worked with distributed teams across Europe, North America and Asia, helping developers trust new tools, adopt new workflows, and build AI systems responsibly. At CBB Analytics, I turned raw basketball data into decisions for some of the biggest programs in US college sport. And at Data Community Africa, I went deep on something harder to measure: what happens when an entire continent of builders finally has a room of their own.

The tension between what's technically possible and who actually gets access to it is what drives everything I do. If you're building something that needs to last, something technically sound, community-rooted, and built for the world as it actually is, that's the work I show up for.
